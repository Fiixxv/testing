The Veilfall

✦ ✦ ✦

Long before humans knew the danger, a barrier separated our world from something older, patient, and unknowable. Scholars call it the Veil. It was invisible, silent, and protective — until it fell.

The Veil did not tear. It fell. And with its collapse, the first of the Veilborn stepped through. Humanity did not notice until it was too late.

The collapse was not a single night. It was a period of weeks, months, and years — shadows slipping through cracks, cities left untouched yet unknowingly threatened, memories warping into reality.

The Veilborn

✦ ✦ ✦

The Veilborn are manifestations of fear, obsession, rage, and forgotten memories. They are adaptive predators. Some are instinct-driven, others cunning. Some even learn from hunters’ strategies, evolving with each encounter.

No two Veilborn are alike. Each one remembers. Each one waits. Some hunger. Some deceive. All hunt.

Over time, the Veilborn grew bold. They began to coordinate attacks, hunt in packs, and even corrupt weak humans into agents of the Veil. The balance of power shifted. The Veilborn were no longer anomalies — they became an organized threat.

The Hunters

✦ ✦ ✦

Hunters rose in response. Not chosen by kings, gods, or fate. They were survivors of early Veilborn encounters — the rare humans who lived through things that should have ended them.

Hunters are forged in darkness. Every scar is a lesson. Every loss is cataloged. Every victory is survival, not glory.

They train in secret, form guilds beyond human oversight, and study the Veilborn meticulously. Every hunt is a battle, every encounter a test. They fight not for recognition, but to prevent humanity from falling entirely.

Conflict Across the Veil

✦ ✦ ✦

The world after the Veilfall is a war without official battlefields. Hunters and Veilborn clash constantly, each shaping the other. The Veilborn grow smarter, stronger, more devious. The hunters adapt, develop new tactics, and endure.

This is not a simple hunter versus monster scenario. Each side evolves from the other. Each side leaves marks, scars, and lessons. This ledger preserves the record of that war.

Sometimes a hunter falls. Sometimes a Veilborn is destroyed. But the war never ends. Every night, the battlefield shifts — in cities, forests, and hidden corners of the world — unseen by ordinary eyes.

The Ledger

✦ ✦ ✦

This archive documents both sides:

  • The Veilborn — their nature, habits, adaptive traits, and known appearances.
  • The Hunters — their abilities, training, scars, ranks, and survival outcomes.
"The ledger is neutral. It records the world as it is, not as we wish it to be."
— A nameless hunter, margins of a fractured guild ledger

Rank and Survival

✦ ✦ ✦

Hunters are ranked based on the Veilborn encounters they survived, the strategies employed, and the lives saved — or lost. From E Rank (initiates barely past their first hunt) to S Rank (rare survivors of countless high-risk encounters).

S Rank does not signify strength. It signifies endurance. It signifies surviving when the world itself is against you. Those marked S are not legends. They are the last witnesses to the Veilfall.

Every entry in this ledger serves a single purpose: to teach, to warn, and to document the ongoing war between the hunters and the Veilborn — a war that is far from over.

The Veilborn Tiers

✦ ✦ ✦

While hunters are measured by rank, the Veilborn are classified by danger. Each manifestation is cataloged according to its lethality, cunning, and adaptability. From minor anomalies to apex predators, these classifications guide the hunters’ approach.

Level I — weak, instinct-driven, usually solitary.
Level II — capable of learning, occasionally cooperative.
Level III — dangerous, adaptive, often hunting in packs.
Level IV — intelligent, deceptive, capable of manipulating humans.
Level V — apex manifestation, rare, highly strategic, sometimes legendary.

These tiers are not permanent. A Level II can become Level III with experience. The ledger records every known encounter, giving hunters a chance to anticipate the evolving threat.

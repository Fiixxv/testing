The Veilfall

✦ ✦ ✦

Long before humans knew the danger, a barrier separated our world from something older, patient, and unknowable. Scholars call it the Veil — invisible, silent, and immovable for millennia. It was not built by hands. It was a wound the world had closed over itself, sealing away something it could not destroy. Then, without warning, it healed wrong.

The Veil did not tear. It fell.
And with its collapse, the first of the Veilborn stepped through. Humanity did not notice until the shadows began to think.

The collapse was not a single catastrophic night. It was a slow unraveling — shadows slipping through invisible fractures, cities left untouched yet quietly infected, memories warping into physical form. By the time scholars named it the Veilfall, it had already been happening for years.

The Veilborn

✦ ✦ ✦

The Veilborn are not creatures. They are consequences. Born from the accumulated weight of fear, obsession, grief, and fury — emotions strong enough to crystallize into form. They do not eat. They do not age. They simply persist, sharpening themselves against the world that rejected them.

No two Veilborn are alike. Each one remembers something. Each one waits for something.
Some hunger. Some deceive. Some simply watch. All of them are patient in ways humans cannot comprehend.

The Hunters

✦ ✦ ✦

Hunters rose in response — not chosen by kings, gods, or fate. They were survivors. The rare humans who walked into something that should have consumed them, and walked back out changed. No one handed them a title. They took it because no one else would.

Hunters are not made in training halls. They are forged in encounters that should have been final.
Every scar is a lesson. Every loss is cataloged. Every victory is survival, never glory.

Hunter Rank

✦ ✦ ✦

Hunters are ranked not by strength, but by survival. From E Rank (initiates past their first hunt) to S Rank (rare survivors of encounters that should have been final).

S Rank does not signify power. It signifies endurance.
Those marked S are not legends. They are the last witnesses.

Veilborn Classification — Dread Tier

✦ ✦ ✦

While hunters are ranked by survival, the Veilborn are classified by their Dread Tier — a measure of lethality, cunning, and resistance to conventional hunting. "Level" is reserved for hunter attributes alone. Veilborn are beyond such simple measurement.

Dread Tier I — Fragile, instinct-driven, usually solitary.
Dread Tier II — Capable of learning, occasionally coordinating.
Dread Tier III — Adaptive, hunting in packs. C-rank response or above.
Dread Tier IV — Intelligent, deceptive, long-term manipulation. Multi-hunter required.
Dread Tier V — Apex manifestation. Rare. Highly strategic. S-rank response only.

Record of the Veilfall

✦ ✦ ✦

"When the Veil fell, the world did not end. It became a battlefield."

The Great Fracture

✦ ✦ ✦

For eons, the Veil held—a silent, shimmering barrier separating the mortal Overworld from the Abyssal Weald. But the Weald is a realm of sentient entropy, fed by the weight of every fear and atrocity committed in the light. When the density of malice reached its singularity, the Veil did not tear; it shattered like glass. This was the Veilfall—a collapse of physical law.

The sky did not bleed;
it broke.
And from the jagged fractures, the first Veilborn descended to claim a world that had forgotten how to scream.

The Whispering Monoliths

✦ ✦ ✦

The pillars did not descend in plain sight. They are the Whispering Monoliths—shards of the original Veil buried in the forgotten corners of the earth. They are exceptionally rare; many Awakened live and die without ever seeing one. To find a Monolith is to stand before an unspoken Law and offer a Sacred Vow.

The Monoliths grant powers that defy reason—from the manipulation of blood and shadow to the folding of time and stars. But the Vow is a path of absolute equilibrium: you cannot receive what you do not pay for. You receive the Law, but you give your humanity. You do not just use the power; you become the price.

The Veilborn: Sin-Eaters

✦ ✦ ✦

The creatures of the Weald are Veilborn—manifestations of the "Too Much." They do not take Vows; they evolve by gorging on the Seven Sins of the Overworld. By absorbing the essence of fear, wrath, and pride, they mutate into higher forms of lethality.

Unlike the Awakened, the Veilborn seek to destroy the Monoliths. When a Veilborn shatters a Monolith, they perform The Ravaging—absorbing the raw, leaked power of the pillar. They only receive 50% of the potential power, but they pay no price and make no sacrifice. They are thieves of reality, growing stronger by breaking the world's remaining anchors.

The Awakened: The Reapers

✦ ✦ ✦

Humanity survived through The Reaping. The Awakened are those who have learned to consume the Astral Essence of slain Veilborn. By drawing the smoke of a monster's death into their own souls, they grow stronger, faster, and more resilient. While a Monolith Vow is a shortcut to godhood, most Awakened rely on the slow, bloody grind of the hunt, sharpening their spirits one kill at a time.

We are not heroes; we are scavengers of the abyss. Every kill is a meal, and every breath of essence is a step away from the light. We hunt the Veilborn to stay human, even as their blood turns our souls into something else entirely.

The Iron Inquisition

✦ ✦ ✦

They do not bargain with Monoliths like the Archive, nor do they steal power through destruction like the Veilborn. The Iron Inquisition follows the Pyre Covenant. They capture fragments of Monoliths or essence from slain Veilborn and ritually burn them upon sacred iron pyres. The purified power is then forged into their armor, weapons, and flesh through flame and iron.

This is their path to power — controlled, disciplined, and pure. But every binding demands a tithe. They burn away their own emotions, memories, and years of life. The stronger an Inquisitor becomes, the less human they remain — cold, unbreakable instruments of absolute order.

The Great Conflict

✦ ✦ ✦

The world is a triptych of violence. The Resonant Archive hunts for Monoliths to ascend through Vows, while The Iron Inquisition hunts the Awakened to prevent the world's sanity from further fraying. All the while, the Veilborn hunt both, seeking to shatter every Monolith and drown the Overworld in the Sins of the Weald.

Between the Archive's hunger for godhood and the Inquisition's fear of the dark, the world burns. We fight over the scraps of a broken reality while the Veilborn laugh from the shadows of our own sins.

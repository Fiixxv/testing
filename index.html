— TOME I —

Record of the Veilfall

✦ ✦ ✦

"When the Veil fell, the world did not end. It became a ledger."

The Great Fracture

✦ ✦ ✦

The Veil was never a shield. It was a membrane of compressed consequence. Beneath it, the Abyssal Weald acted as a pressure gauge: every unchecked cruelty, every bloodletting, every sin left unpunished added weight to a ledger humanity forgot it was writing. The architects of the Veil severely overestimated our restraint.

When the threshold broke, the Veil did not tear. It shattered simultaneously across every sky. Year Zero. The Veilfall. It was not an invasion; it was an eviction notice. The things that poured through the fractures were not foreign conquerors. They were returning residents, coming to claim the misery we had built for them.

The Whispering Monoliths

✦ ✦ ✦

Seventy-seven petrified spines of absolute Law. The Monoliths are the shattered skeleton of the Veil, jutting from the earth like scorched bone. They are silent until they choose a candidate. To stand before one is to feel the crushing gravity of a Vow already decided for you.

The Monolith does not negotiate, and it does not accept symbolic sacrifices. It demands the surrender of something load-bearing from your soul—something that, once severed, leaves you permanently structurally compromised. The Law does not choose the worthy. It chooses those willing to be unmade.

The Resonance Debt

✦ ✦ ✦

Before the Fracture, the Veil quietly collected the price of power. Now, the ledger is broken. Every activation of a Vow, every invocation of the Pyre, crystallizes a fragment of the practitioner and shatters it. Mid-stride. Without warning. This is the Resonance Debt.

It does not take what you can spare. It takes an entire branch of attunement. The memory of your mother's face. The neurological instinct to breathe. You never know when the Debt will collect, only that the subtraction is absolute. You survive by fighting with the pieces of yourself that remain.

The Veilborn: Sin-Eaters

✦ ✦ ✦

They are humanity reflected through a lens stripped of mercy. A Veilborn emerges as a silhouette of compressed sin, evolving through the Seven Apostasies. With each phase, they discard mindless wrath for cold, terrifying precision. They are not mindless beasts; they are a philosophical argument, and they are winning.

They do not destroy the Monoliths. Shattering the Law would leave the world devoid of power, starving them. Instead, they seek to Eclipse them. Veilborn feed on the friction between divine Law and mortal frailty. When an Apex Veilborn claims a Monolith, they invert its resonance—warping a pillar of absolute order into a bleeding font of Abyssal mutation. They do not want to end the world. They want to wear its bones.

The Awakened: Eidolons

✦ ✦ ✦

They begin as human. That is the tragedy of their existence. To fight the Veilborn, one must consume their residue—Astral Essence. But the human vessel was not made to hold the Abyss. With every extraction, the essence does not simply empower the hunter; it displaces their humanity.

By Rank B, their movements mirror the unnatural stillness of their prey. By Rank S, they are Eidolons: entities that wear human skin merely as a residual habit. We are not saviors. We are the predictable outcome of desperate arithmetic. Every breath of essence drawn shifts the scale—more of what kills the Veilborn, less of what makes us worth saving.

The Iron Inquisition: The Tethered

✦ ✦ ✦

If Eidolons Vow to the Monoliths and receive power at the cost of their inner self, the Inquisition refuses the transaction entirely. To Vow is to beg. To stand before a broken god and ask it to name your price is, in the Inquisition's doctrine, the second fall — a voluntary surrender to the same framework that allowed the Veilfall in the first place. They do not Vow. They Bind.

The Pyre Binding is the Inquisition's answer to the Monolith's authority. They take Veilborn essence — the raw residue of sin made physical — and sacrifice it to the sacred flame. What the Pyre returns is not a gift. It is a forged instrument: Iron attunement, Solar attunement, Lunar attunement, power extracted through violence and cold discipline, grafted directly onto the practitioner's soul. No Monolith intermediary. No negotiation. No Vow. They take what they need by burning what they hate.

The Inquisition does not call this free. They call it honest. The Eidolons lose pieces of themselves — their memories, their senses, the architecture of who they are — while the world still holds them. The Tethered lose something different. Each Binding does not reach inward. It reaches outward. The Pyre's Tithe is extracted from the world's relationship to them: not who they are, but who they are to everyone else. Binding by Binding, the world forgets to hold them — not their name, not their face, but the part of itself that was supposed to care.

This is the Pyre's Tithe. The Tethered remain whole. They remember everything. They feel everything — until, at sufficient Bindings, even feeling becomes academic, because there is no one left in the world who feels anything back. By Rank A, a Tethered soldier who has fought beside a comrade for a decade will find that comrade describing them in letters home as "one of the officers." Not coldly. Not cruelly. Simply — with the mild accuracy of someone who cannot locate the weight of why they ever felt differently. The information survives. The attachment does not.

The Great Conflict

✦ ✦ ✦

Three factions. Three answers to a broken world. The Archive chases ascension — Vowing to Monoliths, accepting the catastrophic personal cost the Law names for them, then living with the Resonance Debt that slowly erases whoever they were between activations. The Inquisition chases severance — refusing the Vow, Binding instead, paying in the world's memory of them rather than their memory of themselves. The Veilborn simply consume.

The Archive loses itself and the world still holds them. The Inquisition holds itself and the world loses them. Both factions find the other's condition comprehensible in theory and unbearable in practice, and neither has ever said so directly. There is an argument, whispered quietly, that this stalemate is the only equilibrium left — that as long as the war remains infinite, the world remains unbroken. The first scholar to write this hypothesis lost his ability to perceive sound to the Resonance Debt. The Archive filed the thesis anyway. No Tethered has submitted a counter-thesis. The Archive has noted that no one in the Inquisition would remember filing it.

The Seven Apostasies

✦ ✦ ✦

— Archive Classification Document · Filed under: Veilborn Evolutionary Biology · Access: Rank B and above —

A Veilborn is born empty of direction, not capacity. Phase I entities are already more lethal than most Rank-B Eidolons — they are simply indiscriminate. What transforms a Veilborn from mindless force into something that makes decisions is the absorption of human emotional residue in sufficient density. The Archive calls these evolutionary stages the Seven Apostasies, named for the seven moral failures they correspond to. The naming was deliberate: you cannot study what you refuse to acknowledge as familiar.

PHASE I — WRATH Pure reflex. Destruction as the primary expression of existence. Phase I Veilborn cannot distinguish between targets — everything within proximity is engaged. They cannot retreat because retreat requires intention, and intention requires a self. Roughly 60% of all active Veilborn never evolve past this phase. The Archive considers this fortunate. They are correct.
PHASE II — PRIDE Selection begins. The Veilborn develops a preference for prey that resists — that fights back, that has something worth breaking. Phase II entities bypass weak targets to reach stronger opponents. They have begun to understand hierarchy. They are simply placing themselves at its top through force.
PHASE III — ENVY Observation begins. Phase III entities have been documented watching Eidolons for extended periods before engaging — studying movement, Vow-power deployment, social structures. They want what the Eidolons have. Since they cannot obtain it through exchange, they obtain it through consumption. This is when Veilborn first begin Eclipsing Monoliths directly.
PHASE IV — GLUTTONY Consumption becomes compulsive. A Phase IV entity no longer simply hunts — it absorbs everything within reach, including things it cannot use. Monolith Eclipsing becomes catastrophic at this phase, not from strategy but from the simple inability to remain near a power source without corrupting it. Xyron reached Phase IV at three times the documented average rate. The Archive flagged this. Then lost the file.
PHASE V — SLOTH Counterintuitively the most dangerous early phase. The Veilborn becomes still. It has fed enough to develop patience — and patience in a Veilborn is more destabilizing than rage. Phase V entities select territory, establish positions, and wait. A Phase V entity watching a settlement for three weeks before moving is not resting. It is preparing with a precision its previous phases did not have access to.
PHASE VI — LUST Singular obsession crystallizes. The Veilborn identifies one target — a specific hunter, a specific Monolith, a specific city — and pursues it with total exclusion of everything else. Other Veilborn, Inquisition battalions, Archive strike teams: all are obstacles between it and what it has decided to own. No Phase VI Veilborn has ever been successfully redirected. The Archive has stopped trying.
PHASE VII — GREED Theoretical. No confirmed Phase VII entity has been destroyed and documented. The Archive's models suggest it would cease to resemble an organism and begin to resemble a condition — a geological event rather than a creature. Identification criteria were drafted. The field teams assigned to draft them did not file subsequent reports. The document remains open. The Archive has not assigned new teams.

The Recorded Debts & The Pyre's Tithe

✦ ✦ ✦

— Dual Registry: Resonance Debt Losses (Eidolon) · Pyre's Tithe Severances (Tethered) · Open Archive · Updated continuously —

Both factions pay. They pay differently, and neither considers the other's payment a mercy. The Resonance Debt extracts from within — attunements, memories, senses, the architecture of self — while the world still holds the Eidolon in its care. The Pyre's Tithe extracts from without — the world's capacity to feel anything about the Tethered, Binding by Binding, until they stand entirely intact in a world that can no longer locate the weight of them. The following entries are drawn from both registries. The Archive files them together. Neither faction has objected, because neither faction has been asked.

Archivist Mireille Chen · Rank A · Resonance Debt Loss #114 Lost her entire Stellar attunement. Not degraded — erased. Fourteen years of cultivation, gone in the space between one activation and the next. She was a dual-sovereign practitioner before the Debt collected. She is now single-class. The Archive reclassified her combat rating. She submitted an appeal. The Debt does not process appeals. She remains in service because she is still capable enough to be useful, and the Archive requires useful things. Whether she considers this arrangement adequate has not been documented, because no one asked.
Eidolon Yusuf, "The Ironwood" · Rank S · Resonance Debt Loss #3 of an Unknown Total Lost all somatic sensation. Not warmth — everything. Pain, pressure, temperature, the rhythm of his own heartbeat. He cannot detect injury without looking. During the Siege of Crestfall, he fought for six hours with a weapon lodged between his third and fourth rib. He discovered it four days later during a routine physical. His combat logs describe that engagement as unremarkable. The Archive has noted that an Eidolon who cannot feel death approaching is both extraordinarily effective and extraordinarily difficult to keep alive. Yusuf has not asked them to try harder. He has also not asked what the first two losses were. He says he prefers not to know.
Hunter-Archivist Noa Voss · Rank A · Resonance Debt Loss #8 Lost the ability to form new memories of faces. Everyone she knew before Loss #8 remains intact. Everyone she has met since is a stranger every morning. Her partner of three years introduces himself to her each day. She has built a system: a book, four hundred and eighteen pages, a photograph and written description of every person in her operational life. She reads it before leaving her quarters each morning. Her colleagues describe her as unusually methodical. She has not explained why. She has not explained it because doing so would require her to acknowledge, every day, what was taken — and she has decided that is not a sustainable practice. The Archive has recorded this as "adaptive coping." She has not been asked whether she agrees with the classification.
Tethered Commander Alia Vost · Rank A · Pyre's Tithe · 89 Bindings Confirmed Has not lost any faculty. Her mind is sharp. Her body is intact. Her attunements are performing above baseline. What the Pyre has taken instead is the world's capacity to feel the weight of her. Her unit of six years knows her name, her rank, her record. What they cannot locate is why any of it should mean something. Her second-in-command — a man who once delivered a eulogy for her when she was believed dead, who wept in front of four hundred soldiers — wrote home last month and described her as "the Commander." No name. No anecdote. No grief or gratitude attached. Just a title. She has read the letter. She has not responded to it. She continues to execute her duties with flawless competence because competence, at least, does not require anyone to care.
Tethered Recruit Hend Moro · Rank C · Pyre's Tithe · 3 Bindings Confirmed Three Bindings performed over eleven days. Came to his superior after the third to report that his family had stopped writing — not that they were angry, but that the letters became shorter, then briefer, then a single line: "Hope you are well." His mother, who had written four pages weekly for the duration of his training, sent seven words last month. He asked his superior if this was normal. His superior confirmed that it was. His superior did not tell him it would continue. His superior said: it gets manageable. His superior has performed 203 Bindings and receives no letters at all. He described this, when asked, as clarifying. He did not elaborate. He did not need to. The Pyre's Tithe is not a mystery to anyone who has survived enough of it. It is simply the price printed in a language the recruits learn to read only after they have already paid the first installment.
Grand Inquisitor Severin Cole · Rank S · Pyre's Tithe · 8 Unlogged Bindings, Confirmed Lost the ability to sleep. Not the desire — the mechanism. His body does not enter unconscious rest. He has not slept in eleven years. The Pyre's Tithe has, in parallel, removed him so completely from the world's emotional record that his own subordinates now describe him in after-action reports with the detachment usually reserved for weather conditions. He is present. He is effective. He is acknowledged. But the part of human cognition that assigns personal significance to another person finds nothing to hold when it reaches for him. Cole describes his condition as clarifying. He has described it this way, in identical phrasing, every time he has been asked, for eleven years. His subordinates have stopped asking. Not because they fear his answer. Because they have forgotten why the answer would matter.

The Archive continues to track both registries under the working thesis that neither cost is arbitrary — that the Debt and the Tithe are two faces of the same broken ledger, extracting from different directions toward the same terminal outcome. They have not published this thesis. The Eidolon researcher assigned to it subsequently lost the attunement she used to study it. The Tethered researcher assigned to it was removed from the project when the Archive realized no one on staff could reliably remember why he had been assigned to it in the first place. The documents remain open. The Archive has noted the symmetry. They have not stopped.
